Goodbye Bafana (2007)

Dutch
James Gregory (Joseph Fiennes), een racistische Zuid-Afrikaanse blanke,
krijgt de opdracht om een belangrijke gevangene te bewaken op Robben Island.
Gedurende de twintig jaar waarin zij met elkaar te maken krijgen,
groeit er een band tussen de twee mannen, ondanks het feit dat de gevangene een neger is.
De naam van de gevangene is Nelson Mandela (Dennis Haysbert).

Englisch
James Gregory once lived in a farm and had befriended a native youth,
Bafana, and had even had a photograph taken with him. Years later,
now married to Gloria and father of three children (Chris, Brett, and Natasha),
James has nothing but shame and regret, as many South African Caucasians
in the oppressive Apartheid-era ridiculed him, leading him to hate Africans.
He seeks to redeem himself by spying on imprisoned African National Congress Leader,
Nelson Mandela. In the restrictive high security prison his job is to censor
all written and verbal communications between prisoners, their visitors,
and correspondence. James is uncomfortable when he witnesses Caucasian police
and security officers' brutality against civilians, including infants,
and tries to understand why Nelson became a rebel. This leads him to examine the
'Freedom Charter', a banned document, reportedly known to incite violence against 'whites'...
