Album Info

Word of Mouf is the second studio album by American rapper Ludacris. It was released on November 27, 2001 by Def Jam South. Def Jam South decided to release the album early when pirated copies of the album's tracks were leaked onto popular peer-to-peer file sharing networks such as LimeWire.

The album debuted at number three US Billboard 200 chart, and sold over 281,000 copies in its first-week of sales, The album has become a certified three-times platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). The album is Ludacris' best-selling album to date with sales of over 3,616,000 copies in the US as of July, 2009. It contains the singles, "Rollout (My Business)", "Area Codes", "Move Bitch", and "Saturday (Oooh Oooh!)". It was nominated at the Grammy Awards of 2003 for Best Rap Album, but lost to The Eminem Show.
