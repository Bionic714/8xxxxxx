'The Stone Flower' or 'Kammenny Tsvetok' is a Russian fantasy film made in 1946 by Alexander Ptushko. Ptushko was more known for his animation films and was even called the Walt Disney of Russia. 

This, incidentally is the first ever color film to come out of U.S.S.R and was made shot on Agfacolor negative film seized in Germany. It was entered into the 1946 Cannes Film Festival and won a "special prize for the use of color". 

It is based on a folktale by Pavel Bazhov.
