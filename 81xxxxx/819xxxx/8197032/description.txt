Talvisota (The Winter War) is perhaps the most realistic war movie ever produced.
It was released in 1989, at the 50th anniversary of the events that it depicts.

Set during World War 2. After the Nazi Germany invaded Poland in September 1939, Russia invaded Finland in November 1939. Finnish reservists leave their homes and go to war. The film focuses on two farmers from the municipality of Kauhava in the province of Pohjanmaa/Ostrobothnia, brothers Martti and Paavo Hakala, serving in a Finnish platoon.
