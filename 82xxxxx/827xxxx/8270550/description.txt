Backbeat is a 1994 British-German drama film directed by Iain Softley. 

It chronicles the early days of The Beatles in Hamburg, Germany. 

The film focuses primarily on the relationship betweenStuart Sutcliffe (Stephen Dorff) and John Lennon (Ian Hart), and also with Sutcliffe's German girlfriend Astrid Kirchherr (Sheryl Lee).
