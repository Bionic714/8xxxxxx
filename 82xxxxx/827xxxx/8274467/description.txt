Stawka większa niż życie (eng. Playing for High Stakes) was a very popular Polish black and white TV series about the adventures of a Polish secret agent, Hans Kloss (real name, Stanisław Kolicki, codename J-23), who acts as a double agent in the Abwehr during Second World War in occupied Poland.


The series was filmed from March 1967 to October 1968. There were 18 episodes, 9 of which were directed by Janusz Morgenstern and the other 9 by Andrzej Konic.
It became popular also in other countries, especially in former Czechoslovakia.
