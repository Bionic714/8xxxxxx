Wael Ghonim (Arabic: وائل غنيم‎, IPA: [ˈwæːʔel ɣoˈneːm]; other transliterations include: Ghoneim, Ghonaim) (born 23 December 1980 in Cairo, Egypt) is an Internet activist and computer engineer with an interest in social entrepreneurship.[2]

In 2011, he became an international figure and energized pro-democracy demonstrations in Egypt after his emotional interview[3] following 11 days of secret incarceration by Egyptian police—during which he was interrogated regarding his work as the administrator of thepage, "We are all Khaled Saeed", which helped spark the revolution.[4][5] TIME magazine included him in its "Time 100" list of 100 most influential people of 2011.[6]
