Stunning Saturn (BBC)

Science Documentary hosted by Chris Lintott and Lucie Green, published by BBC broadcasted as part of BBC The Sky at Night series in 2013 - English narration


Information
------------------------------

With Saturn in our evening skies, Lucie Green and Chris Lintott investigate the storm that is still raging in the planet's atmosphere, with the latest news from Saturn's amazing moons Titan and Enceladus. Pete Lawrence and Paul Abel illustrate Saturn's 'opposition effect' and look at some globular clusters, whilst Chris North gets a preview of the new eye-in-the-sky camera, soon to be fitted onto the International Space Station, which will image Earth in incredible detail.

30 minute SD edition is broadcast on 9th May - there's no 30 minute HD edition.
There's also a 2007 broadcast of Sky at Night with the same name "Stunning Saturn".
