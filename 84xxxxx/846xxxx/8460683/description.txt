Mughal-E-Azam (English: The Greatest of the Mughals) is a 1960 Indian period epic film starring Prithviraj Kapoor, Dilip Kumar & Madhubala. It was directed by K.Asif and produced by Shapoorni Pallonji. Mughal-E-Azam is widely considered a classic, and is often recognized as a milestone in Indian cinema.

The music director for the film's soundtrack was Naushad, and the lyrics were penned by Shakeel Badayuni. The music was heavily inspired from Indian classical and folk music. The soundtrack contained 12 songs, voiced by both playback singers such as Lata Mangeshkar, Shamshad Begum, Mohammad Rafi, and classical music artists. It is often cited as one of the best soundtracks in Bollywood history.
